1. THE LARGEST RIVER IN THE WORLD. AMAZON: 6800 KM For a long time, the Nile was considered the largest river, but in 2007, Brazilian scientists discovered a new starting point for the Amazon - a stream flowing from a glacier on Mount Mismi. The area of ​​the Amazon Basin is 6.9 million km2, which is about twice the size of India. It flows through Brazil, Peru, Bolivia, Colombia, Ecuador, Venezuela, Guyana. And its length can be compared with the distance between St. Petersburg and Chita by car. In addition, the Amazon is the deepest river in the world. The average volume of water discharged into the Atlantic Ocean is 219,000 m3/s.

2. NIL: 6670 km The river was formed about 30 million years ago, and the inhabitants of Ancient Egypt called it lteru - the source of life. It was the floods of the Nile, which applied a fertile layer of silt to the adjacent lands, making them suitable for agriculture. In addition to Egypt, the Nile covers the territories of Burundi, Kenya, Congo, Rwanda, Sudan, South Sudan, Tanzania, Uganda, Eritrea, and Ethiopia. If the Nile were in Russia, it could flow from Bryansk to Chita. The area of the river system is 3.3 million km2 - about the size of India. The volume of water is 5100 m3/s.
